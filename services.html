General Constructing

We help our clients with complete construction packages which can range from land acquisition to actual construction through to completion. Through our wide network in the construction industry, we can arrange for Architectural, Quantity Surveying and Engineering services for any of our clients incase we are asked to. At Jenga Construction we take cognizance of the idiom that “time is money”. We therefore undertake construction on tight schedules............

Building Remodeling

Remodeling describes the situation where an owner of a building wants to change aspects of an existing building to accommodate new needs. These may include the desire to create more space, the need for modern amenities or even the desire to change the arrangement of rooms. Our Engineering staff, equipped with the thorough knowledge of material choices and designs, will advise the client as to what remodeling to adopt. We undertake both interior and exterior remodeling of all types of buildings.

Renovation

The Webster dictionary describes the word ‘renovate’ as to make new or make like new. Indeed, renovating an old home or office, for example, can inspire a feeling of comfort and pleasure which one associates with a new building. At Jenga Construction, we renovate all parts of a building structure, including walls, the floor, the roof, doors, and windows, kitchens, bedrooms, hallways, offices, washrooms, reception areas, electrical installations, plumbing works, air conditioning, etc…… in a bid to add color and improve the total functionality of a home or office.

Restoration

Restoration refers to work undertaken to a building to renew or improve its underlying structure. The trick here is to make sure that the balance is retained between the old and the new by deploying similar materials and construction techniques. Unlike renovation which in some cases may not be life-threatening, restoration addresses fabrics of a building which have failed and may pose danger to life.

Roofing

Apart from foundations, the roof is probably the most neglected part of any building. While the walls will get the periodic coat of paint and doors and windows the occasional scrub, the roof calls for attention only when there is a leak. This is despite the fact that the roof is the most exposed element of a building to erosion and other vagaries of the weather.

Waste Water Recycling

Located as it is on the Coastline of the Indian Ocean, Dar Es Salaam has its water-table high on the surface. As a result, most building owners who dispose off refuge by way of the conventional septic tank, have their pits overflowing all year round. This would explain why, almost without exception, all rivers in Dar Es Salaam are teaming up with foul-smelling sewage. Those urbanites civil enough to empty their pits regularly using tankers are faced with a monthly bill running into thousands of shillings.
